## Overview

Products are the core of your advertising displays in PromoMaster Pro. This guide walks you through adding products to your catalog, configuring their details, and setting up promotional offerings.

## Adding a New Product

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Products Tab">
    From the Advanced Configuration page, click on the **Products** tab to access product management.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Product Identification">
    In the "New Product" form, enter the product name in the **Identification** field. This is a required field that will appear prominently in your displays.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Product Image">
    Choose one of three methods to add your product image:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="URL">
        Paste a direct link to an image hosted online:
        ```text
        https://ejemplo.com/foto.jpg
        ```
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Upload">
        Click **Select File** to upload an image from your computer. The system accepts standard image formats (JPG, PNG, etc.).
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="AI Generation">
        Click **Generate with AI** to automatically create a product image based on the product name. 
        
        <Note>The product name must be filled in before generating an AI image.</Note>
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Price and Unit">
    Configure the pricing information:
    - **Price**: Enter the numeric price value
    - **Unit**: Select from available options:
      - Unidad (Unit)
      - Kilo (kg)
      - Gramos (g)
      - Litro (Liter)
      - Pack
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a Slogan (Optional)">
    Create an impactful advertising message for your product. You can:
    - Type your own custom slogan in the text area
    - Click the sparkle icon to **generate a slogan with AI** based on the product name and description

    <Tip>Short, punchy slogans work best on display screens. Aim for 3-8 words.</Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Promotion Settings">
    If this product is on sale, check the **"ES UNA OFERTA"** checkbox. This reveals promotion options:

    <CardGroup cols={3}>
      <Card title="General Offer" icon="tag">
        Standard promotion without time constraints
      </Card>
      <Card title="Daily Offer" icon="calendar-day">
        Highlights the product as today's special deal
      </Card>
      <Card title="Weekly Offer" icon="calendar-week">
        Promotes the product as this week's featured item
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>

    <Note>Promotion badges will automatically appear on the display screens to attract customer attention.</Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the Product">
    Click **Create Product** to add the item to your catalog. The product will immediately appear in the products list and become available for adding to ad sequences.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Existing Products

### Editing a Product

Click the edit icon (pencil) on any product card to load its information into the form. Make your changes and click **Update Product** to save.

### Deleting a Product

Click the trash icon to remove a product from your catalog.

<Warning>Deleting a product is permanent and will remove it from any existing sequences.</Warning>

## Best Practices

<Accordion title="Image Quality">
  Use high-resolution images (at least 800x800 pixels) for the best display quality. Images should have good contrast and be clearly visible from a distance.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pricing Strategy">
  Keep prices competitive and clearly visible. For promotional items, ensure the discount is significant enough to drive customer interest.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Effective Slogans">
  Focus on benefits, not features. Examples:
  - "Fresh Every Day"
  - "Premium Quality, Unbeatable Price"
  - "Your Family Deserves the Best"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Color Selection">
  The primary color field affects the visual theme of your ad displays. Choose colors that:
  - Match your brand identity
  - Create good contrast with white text
  - Stand out on digital screens
</Accordion>
