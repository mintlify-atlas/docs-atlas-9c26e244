## Overview

Store management in PromoMaster Pro allows you to configure multiple retail locations, each with its own advertising displays. This is essential for businesses with multiple branches or departments.

## Adding a New Store

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Stores Tab">
    From the Advanced Configuration page, click on the **Stores** tab (Sucursales).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Store Name">
    In the "New Store" form, enter a unique name for your store location. This name will appear on advertising displays.

    <Tip>Use clear, recognizable names like "Downtown Branch" or "North Location" rather than codes or abbreviations.</Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Store Address">
    Enter the complete address for the store. This information appears on product displays to help customers find your location.

    ```text
    Example: 123 Main Street, Shopping District
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the Store">
    Click **Save Store** (Guardar Local) to add the location to your system. The store will immediately be available for use in ad sequences.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding Store Fields

### Store Name
The store name serves as the primary identifier and appears prominently on all advertising displays for that location. Choose names that customers will recognize instantly.

### Store Address
The address is displayed on product advertisements, helping customers locate the store offering the promoted items. Include enough detail for customers to find you easily.

### Logo Color
Each store is assigned a color that appears in the store identifier badge. While this is automatically assigned, it helps visually distinguish between different locations in the management interface.

## Editing Store Information

To update store details:

1. Locate the store in the stores list
2. Click the **edit icon** (pencil) next to the store name
3. Modify the information in the form
4. The form changes from "New Store" to "Edit Store"
5. Click **Save Store** to update

## Deleting a Store

Click the trash icon next to any store to remove it from your system.

<Warning>Deleting a store will affect any active ad sequences assigned to that location.</Warning>

## Multi-Store Scenarios

### Chain Stores

For retail chains with multiple identical locations:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Location-Specific Stores">
    Add each physical location as a separate store with its unique address.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use Consistent Naming">
    Follow a naming convention like:
    - "[Brand] - Downtown"
    - "[Brand] - Mall Location"
    - "[Brand] - Airport Terminal"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Share Product Catalogs">
    Products are shared across all stores, but you can create location-specific ad sequences to highlight different items at different locations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Department Stores

For locations with multiple departments:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Single Store Approach" icon="building">
    Create one store for the entire location and use product categories to organize departments.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multi-Store Approach" icon="grid">
    Create separate "stores" for each department (Grocery, Meat, Produce) if they need independent displays.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Store Selection in Dashboard

When creating ad sequences, you'll select which store will display the content. This is found in the Dashboard's store selector dropdown.

<Note>You must have at least one store configured before you can launch any advertising displays.</Note>

## Best Practices

<Accordion title="Naming Conventions">
  Use consistent, descriptive names:
  - Include geographic identifiers (North, South, Downtown)
  - Keep names concise but clear
  - Avoid special characters that might not display properly
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Address Format">
  Provide complete addresses that customers can use:
  - Include street number and name
  - Add landmark information if helpful
  - Consider including phone numbers in the address field for customer convenience
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Organizational Strategy">
  For large operations:
  - Group stores by region if you have many locations
  - Use a master list to track which stores are active
  - Regularly audit store information for accuracy
</Accordion>
