## Overview

Customization in PromoMaster Pro allows you to control how your advertisements appear on screen. From product colors to announcement typography, these settings help you create visually impactful displays that match your brand.

## Product Display Customization

### Primary Color

Each product has a **Primary Color** field that controls the visual theme when that product is displayed.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Product Settings">
    Edit any product from the Products tab in Advanced Configuration.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Your Color">
    The primary color affects:
    - Background glow effects
    - Brand badge color
    - Accent elements
    - Progress bar color during rotation
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test the Result">
    Launch the product in a display to see how the color appears on screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>Use colors that create strong contrast with white text and dark backgrounds. Vibrant colors (reds, blues, purples) work best for digital displays.</Tip>

### Product Images

Image quality directly impacts visual appeal:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="High Resolution" icon="image">
    Use images at least 800x800px for crisp display quality
  </Card>
  <Card title="Good Lighting" icon="sun">
    Choose well-lit product photos with clear details
  </Card>
  <Card title="Clean Backgrounds" icon="square">
    White or transparent backgrounds work best
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Slogans

Product slogans appear in italic text below the product name with the primary color:

```text
Example: "Fresh From Our Farms Daily"
```

For maximum impact:
- Keep slogans under 8 words
- Use action-oriented language
- Highlight unique benefits

## Announcement Customization

Announcements offer extensive visual customization options. Access these from the **Free Announcements** tab.

### Typography Settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Font Family">
    Choose from four font options:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Impactante (Bebas)">
        **Best for:** Bold statements, prices, headlines
        
        **Style:** Condensed, all-caps, high-impact
        
        **Use cases:**
        - "GRAND OPENING"
        - "50% OFF THIS WEEK"
        - "NEW HOURS"
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Limpio (Inter)">
        **Best for:** General announcements, body text
        
        **Style:** Clean, modern, highly readable
        
        **Use cases:**
        - Store policies
        - Operating hours
        - General information
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Elegante (Serif)">
        **Best for:** Premium messaging, sophisticated brands
        
        **Style:** Classic, traditional, refined
        
        **Use cases:**
        - Gourmet product announcements
        - Quality statements
        - Upscale branding
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Técnico (Mono)">
        **Best for:** Technical information, data displays
        
        **Style:** Monospace, technical, precise
        
        **Use cases:**
        - Technical specifications
        - System messages
        - Data presentations
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Adjust Font Size">
    Use the slider to set text size from **20px to 200px**.

    <Note>The preview shows approximately 1/3 scale. Actual display size will be 3x larger.</Note>

    **Recommended sizes:**
    - Short headlines: 120-200px
    - Medium announcements: 80-120px
    - Detailed messages: 60-80px
    - Fine print: 40-60px
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Text Alignment">
    Choose alignment using the button controls:

    <CardGroup cols={3}>
      <Card title="Left" icon="align-left">
        Formal, traditional layouts
      </Card>
      <Card title="Center" icon="align-center">
        Balanced, attention-grabbing (most common)
      </Card>
      <Card title="Right" icon="align-right">
        Unique, artistic layouts
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Color Settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Background Color">
    Click the **Background** color picker to select your announcement's background color.

    <Tip>Dark backgrounds (navy, black, deep purple) make text stand out on bright screens.</Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose Text Color">
    Click the **Text** color picker to select your message color.

    Common combinations:
    ```text
    White text on dark blue background
    Yellow text on black background
    Dark text on bright yellow background
    White text on red background (urgent messages)
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify Contrast">
    Use the real-time preview to ensure your text is clearly readable against the background.

    <Warning>Low contrast combinations (e.g., light gray on white) will be difficult to read from a distance.</Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Preview Window

The announcement editor includes a **Real-Time Preview** that shows exactly how your announcement will appear:

- Background and text colors update instantly
- Font changes are reflected immediately
- Text alignment shifts as you adjust it
- Subtle shine animation indicates the animation setting

## Display Effects

### Product Display Effects

Product displays automatically include:

- **Image zoom animation**: Subtle pulsing effect on product images
- **Glow effects**: Dynamic background glows using the primary color
- **Ticker tape**: Scrolling messages at the bottom with store and product info
- **Promotion badges**: Automatic badges for items marked as offers

### Announcement Animations

The **Animation** setting adds a subtle shine effect that sweeps across the announcement periodically, drawing viewer attention.

## Visual Impact Tips

<Steps>
  <Step title="Contrast is King">
    Always ensure maximum contrast between text and backgrounds. If viewers squint, you've failed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Limit Color Palette">
    Use 2-3 colors maximum per announcement. Too many colors create visual chaos.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Font Hierarchy">
    Use larger, bolder fonts (Bebas) for headlines and smaller, cleaner fonts (Inter) for details.
  </Step>

  <Step title="White Space">
    Don't fill every pixel. Empty space helps important elements stand out.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test at Distance">
    View your displays from 10-15 feet away (typical customer viewing distance) to verify readability.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Maintain consistent styling across your displays:

<CardGroup cols={2}>

  </Card>
  <Card title="Typography Rules" icon="font">

  </Card>
</CardGroup>
